Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a referral to see a rheumatologist?

In many cases, a referral from your primary care physician is recommended and may be required by your insurance. Our care coordinators will help you navigate this — just give us a call and we'll confirm what your specific plan requires.

Does insurance cover rheumatology visits?

Yes — we accept most major commercial insurance plans and Medicare. Our team verifies your benefits before your first appointment and handles all prior authorizations so you're never caught off guard by a bill.

What should I bring to my first appointment?

Please bring a list of your current medications, any recent labs or imaging, your insurance card, and a summary of your symptom history. The more context we have from the start, the more productive that first visit will be for you.

How long are appointments?

New patient appointments are typically 45–60 minutes. We build in time to understand your full history and answer your questions — not just review your chart for five minutes and move on.

Can you manage medications I'm already on?

Absolutely. If you're already on a biologic, DMARD, or other rheumatology medication, we can take over management of your existing treatment and evaluate whether adjustments or alternatives are appropriate.

Do you offer on-site infusions for biologics?

Yes. We offer on-site infusion therapy for patients receiving biologic treatments that require IV administration. Infusions are administered in a comfortable, private setting with your care team present throughout.
